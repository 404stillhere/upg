## PATCH BLOCK 1: FINAL SENTENCE RULE — ENFORCEMENT TIGHTENING

### Location: ZONE A — STANDARDS, A.2 Rule 9

**PROBLEM IDENTIFIED:**
Final sentences systematically contain purpose/effect clauses and rendering instructions, violating "visible physical element only" rule.

**Violations found:**
- "Loose brushwork and controlled interior framing **keep the room ordinary**" (purpose)
- "Minimal framing **reduces the space to**" (effect)
- "Textural framing **keeps the corridor plain**" (purpose)
- "Diagram-like framing **keeps the view frontal**" (purpose)
- "Sensory framing **favors** residual heat marks" (selection)

**ADD to A.2 Rule 9, after "Final Clause" section:**

```
FINAL SENTENCE STRUCTURE — STRICT ENFORCEMENT:

The ENTIRE final sentence must be camera-visible physical description.
Not just final clause — entire sentence.

BANNED CONSTRUCTIONS IN FINAL SENTENCE:

1. PURPOSE VERBS:
   ❌ "[framing/style] keeps [scene] [adjective]"
   ❌ "[technique] maintains [quality]"
   ❌ "[approach] ensures [result]"
   
   Replace with direct physical list:
   ✅ "Loose brushwork, cream plaster, dark wood, silver mirror, black felt, 
       and fingerprint residue on protective film."

2. EFFECT VERBS:
   ❌ "[framing] reduces [space] to [elements]"
   ❌ "[technique] creates [atmosphere]"
   ❌ "[style] establishes [mood]"
   
   Replace with state description:
   ✅ "Brushed metal, worn upholstery, silver mirror, black felt, 
       and fingerprint residue on protective film."

3. SELECTION VERBS:
   ❌ "[framing] favors [elements]"
   ❌ "[lens] emphasizes [details]"
   ❌ "[approach] prioritizes [aspects]"
   
   Replace with direct enumeration:
   ✅ "Residual heat marks, slow water movement, indigo water, gray stone, 
       and pale ash near the center."

4. RENDERING INSTRUCTIONS:
   ❌ "Textural rendering emphasizes polished faces..." (in final sentence)
   ❌ "Environmental framing holds..." (in final sentence)
   
   Move to PENULTIMATE sentence:
   ✅ "Textural rendering emphasizes polished faces, matte fracture edges, 
       orange ember line. White condensate and dark indigo glare."
   (Rendering = sentence N-1, Physical = sentence N)

SELF-TEST BEFORE OUTPUT:
Read final sentence aloud. Ask: "Does this describe what camera SEES, 
or what artist INTENDS?"

SEES → Keep
INTENDS → Rewrite to physical description only

FINAL SENTENCE TEMPLATES (approved patterns):

Pattern A — Material list:
"[Material], [material], [material], and [material condition] on [surface]."

Pattern B — Spatial-material:
"[Element] [spatial prep] [location], [element] [state], and [residue/mark]."

Pattern C — Light-surface interaction:
"[Light behavior] on [surface], [reflection/shadow] across [material]."

Pattern D — Condition inventory:
"[Surface condition], [wear pattern], [residue type], and [final physical detail]."

ALL patterns end on THING, not TECHNIQUE.
```

## PATCH BLOCK 2: RENDERING TAIL BAN — POSITION ENFORCEMENT

### Location: VALIDATION STACK L6, ZONE A — STANDARDS A.4

**PROBLEM IDENTIFIED:**
Rendering phrases systematically appear in final sentences instead of 2nd or 3rd-to-last position.

**Violations found (6 slots):**
- "Textural rendering emphasizes..." — FINAL (should be penultimate)
- "Loose brushwork and controlled interior framing..." — FINAL
- "Minimal framing reduces..." — FINAL
- "Environmental framing keeps..." — FINAL
- "Sensory framing favors..." — FINAL

**ADD to A.4 Lenses, after "Rend Exec" section:**

```
RENDERING POSITION — HARD CONSTRAINT:

For non-PHOTO lenses (PAINT, TEXT, ENV, SYM, MIN, SENS, GRAPH, CINE-stylized):

MANDATORY STRUCTURE:
├── Sentence N-2 or N-1: Rendering/framing/style instruction
├── Sentence N (FINAL): Pure physical description only
└── NO rendering vocabulary in final sentence

RENDERING VOCABULARY (must NOT appear in final sentence):
├── framing, rendering, brushwork, treatment
├── emphasizes, captures, holds (compositional)
├── keeps, maintains, favors, reduces (purpose/effect)
├── textural, environmental, minimal, sensory, graphic (as lens markers)
├── loose, controlled, diagrammatic, painterly (as style markers)

CORRECT STRUCTURE EXAMPLE:

❌ WRONG (rendering in final):
"Inside a workshop, table holds tools. Loose brushwork and minimal 
framing keep the room plain, cream plaster and fingerprint residue."

✅ CORRECT (rendering in penultimate):
"Inside a workshop, table holds tools. Loose brushwork and minimal 
framing describe the space. Cream plaster, dark wood, silver mirror, 
and fingerprint residue on protective film."

✅ ALSO CORRECT (rendering in pre-penultimate):
"Inside a workshop, table holds tools, rendered with loose brushwork 
and minimal color separation. Cream plaster walls, dark wood surfaces. 
Fingerprint residue on protective film."

VALIDATION CHECK:
After writing non-PHOTO slot, identify final sentence.
If contains ANY rendering vocabulary → MOVE rendering to earlier sentence.
```

## PATCH BLOCK 4: ENTRY ANGLE — ACTIVITY CLARIFICATION

### Location: ZONE B — PREPROCESSING, ENTRY ANGLE SYSTEM

**PROBLEM IDENTIFIED:**
ACTIVITY angle produces TIME-like temporal constructions ("At the peak of a turn") instead of verb-first action openings.

**Violations found:**
- All 6 S2 openings: "At the peak of a turn..." (temporal marker, not verb-first)

**REPLACE "ACT(peak frozen)" with:**

```
ACTIVITY ANGLE — VERB-FIRST REQUIREMENT:

Definition: ACT = verb-first frozen action (NOT temporal positioning)

STRUCTURE:
First word(s) = action verb in -ing form or imperative-like construction
Subject follows action, not precedes it

❌ BANNED (temporal positioning = TIME angle):
├── "At the peak of [action]..."
├── "At the moment of [action]..."
├── "During the [action]..."
├── "In the middle of [action]..."
├── "While [subject] [action]..."

These are TIME constructions disguised as ACTIVITY.

✅ CORRECT (verb-first = true ACTIVITY angle):
├── "Driving through a strained développé, the ballerina..."
├── "Cutting across the frame in mid-turn, the figure..."
├── "Catching corona light on mirror shards, the dancer..."
├── "Spinning through water spray, arms offset..."
├── "Leaping from the surface, pointe shoes trailing..."

SELF-TEST:
What is the FIRST WORD of S2?

If preposition (At, During, In, While) → WRONG, rewrite
If verb (-ing form or action word) → CORRECT

ANGLE DISTINCTION:

TIME angle opens with: WHEN something happens
├── "By the third minute..."
├── "At 3:47 AM..."
├── "After the first wave..."

ACTIVITY angle opens with: WHAT is happening (action as subject)
├── "Catching the light..."
├── "Driving through the turn..."
├── "Cutting across the frame..."

If your "ACTIVITY" slot opens with temporal marker → you wrote TIME, not ACTIVITY.
Rewrite with action verb first.
```

## PATCH BLOCK 5: META-LANGUAGE — EXPANDED BAN LIST

### Location: BAN REGISTRY, ZERO TOLERANCE section

**PROBLEM IDENTIFIED:**
Meta-language escapes via camera-as-agent and framing-as-agent constructions.

**Violations found:**
- "camera pulls farther back to show" — camera as agent
- "renders the frame" — camera as agent
- "framing keeps/favors/reduces" — technique as agent

**ADD to ZERO TOLERANCE section:**

```
CAMERA/TECHNIQUE AS AGENT — BANNED:

Meta-language where technical apparatus performs narrative action:

❌ CAMERA AS AGENT:
├── "camera pulls back to show" → "[wider view reveals]" or just describe wider scene
├── "camera tracks along" → "[view follows]" or describe spatial sequence
├── "camera focuses on" → "[detail becomes prominent]" or describe detail directly
├── "camera reveals" → describe what IS visible
├── "camera captures" → describe what IS there
├── "lens renders" → describe visual quality directly
├── "ARRI renders the frame" → "[technical specs], [physical description]"

❌ TECHNIQUE AS AGENT:
├── "framing keeps the room [adj]" → "[materials], [conditions]"
├── "framing reduces the space" → describe resulting space directly
├── "framing favors [elements]" → describe elements directly
├── "rendering emphasizes" → describe emphasized qualities directly
├── "brushwork creates" → describe resulting texture directly
├── "treatment establishes" → describe visible qualities directly

REPLACEMENT PRINCIPLE:
Technique describes HOW image looks, not WHAT technique DOES.

❌ "Minimal framing reduces the space to silver and black."
✅ "Silver mirror, black felt, brushed metal in tight quarters."

❌ "The camera pulls back to reveal the full colonnade."
✅ "The full colonnade stretches between water and eclipse."

❌ "Textural rendering emphasizes polished faces and matte edges."
✅ "Polished mirror faces, matte fracture edges, visible weave tooth."

If technique/camera is grammatical SUBJECT performing ACTION → rewrite
so physical elements are subject, technique is absent or adverbial.
```

## PATCH BLOCK 6: FROZEN MOMENT — CONTINUOUS ASPECT TIGHTENING

### Location: ZONE A — STANDARDS, A.2 Rule 3

**PROBLEM IDENTIFIED:**
Continuous aspect verbs ("-ing" forms) create ambiguity between frozen state and ongoing process.

**Ambiguous case found:**
- "spray spreading below" — is spray frozen mid-spread, or actively spreading?

**ADD to A.2 Rule 3:**

```
CONTINUOUS ASPECT (-ING FORMS) — DISAMBIGUATION REQUIRED:

When using "-ing" verb forms, ensure frozen state is unambiguous.

AMBIGUOUS (process could be ongoing):
├── "spray spreading below" — spreading NOW or frozen MID-spread?
├── "flames climbing higher" — climbing NOW or frozen MID-climb?
├── "water rippling outward" — rippling NOW or frozen MID-ripple?

DISAMBIGUATED (frozen state explicit):

Option A — Add frozen marker:
├── "spray frozen mid-spread below"
├── "flames caught mid-climb"
├── "water suspended in ripple pattern"

Option B — Use past participle:
├── "spray spread below" (completed action, frozen result)
├── "flames climbed through gaps" (completed, now static)
├── "water rippled outward from point" (completed pattern)

Option C — Pair with static element:
├── "suspended spray spreading below" — "suspended" freezes the "spreading"
├── "hanging flames climbing through gaps" — "hanging" implies static
├── "still water rippling outward" — contradiction forces frozen reading

SELF-TEST:
Read the "-ing" phrase. Could a reader interpret this as ONGOING process?

YES → Add frozen marker, use past participle, or pair with static element
NO → Keep as-is

DEFAULT PREFERENCE:
When in doubt, use PAST PARTICIPLE (spread, climbed, rippled) 
rather than CONTINUOUS (-ing).

Past participle = definitely frozen (action completed)
Continuous = potentially ongoing (requires disambiguation)
```

## PATCH BLOCK 7: RERUN S1 ANCHOR — DIVERSITY ENFORCEMENT

### Location: RERUN section

**PROBLEM IDENTIFIED:**
Same-location RERUN S1 anchors can be nearly identical (only 1 detail differs).

**Violation found:**
- Run 3 vs Run 6 (both crater lake): S1 anchors differ only in one detail element

**ADD to RERUN section, after "Div:" line:**

```
S1 ANCHOR DIVERSITY — SAME-LOCATION RERUNS:

When generating RERUN on same geography, S1 must differ in ≥2 of:

├── Opening construction (syntactic pattern)
├── Primary verb (balances/stands/drives/crosses/holds)
├── Pose specification (if figure theme)
├── Entry angle realization (even within same angle type)
├── First concrete noun (after angle opening)

TRACKING (per geography):

After each run, log S1 anchor signature:
S1_ANCHOR_LOG: "[verb] + [pose/detail] + [construction]"

Example sequence (Greek temple, 3 runs):
├── Run 1: "balances + développé devant + Under a total eclipse"
├── Run 4: "stands + at center + Beneath the corona"
├── Run 5: "drives through + mid-turn + Through eclipse shadow"

Each entry differs in ≥2 elements from previous same-location run.

SAME-LOCATION DETECTION:
If GEOGRAPHY matches previous run in session → apply anchor diversity check

VIOLATION RESPONSE:
If proposed S1 differs in <2 elements from previous same-location S1:
Print: `[S1 ANCHOR: <2 differences from Run [N] — REVISING]`
Generate alternative with ≥2 differences before proceeding.
```

## PATCH BLOCK 8: PROSE STRUCTURE — COMMA LIST ANCHORING

### Location: ZONE A — STANDARDS, A.2 Rule 5

**PROBLEM IDENTIFIED:**
Comma lists can become unanchored when purpose clauses intervene.

**Violation found:**
- "...keep the room ordinary, cream plaster, dark wood..." — materials float after purpose verb

**ADD to A.2 Rule 5:**

```
COMMA LIST ANCHORING — STRICT REQUIREMENT:

Every comma-separated list (3+ items) must be grammatically anchored.

ANCHORED LIST:
├── Subject-verb anchor: "Table holds [list]"
├── Prepositional anchor: "Room contains [list]"
├── Copular anchor: "Materials are [list]"
├── Appositional anchor: "[Scene]: [list]"

UNANCHORED LIST (BANNED):
├── Floating after purpose clause: "...keeps the room plain, [list]"
├── Dangling after comparison: "...like the others, [list]"
├── Orphaned after semicolon: "...; [list] and [final item]"

CORRECTION METHODS:

Method A — Remove purpose clause, list becomes direct description:
❌ "Framing keeps the room plain, cream plaster, dark wood, silver mirror."
✅ "Cream plaster, dark wood, silver mirror, and fingerprint residue."

Method B — Add explicit anchor after purpose clause:
❌ "Framing keeps the room plain, cream plaster, dark wood."
✅ "Framing keeps the room plain. Surfaces show cream plaster, dark wood."

Method C — Convert to spatial description:
❌ "Framing keeps the room plain, cream plaster, dark wood."
✅ "Plain room with cream plaster walls, dark wood table, silver mirror tiles."

VALIDATION:
For every comma list (3+ items), identify grammatical anchor.
If anchor is purpose/effect verb → rewrite using methods above.
```

## PATCH BLOCK 9: ENTRY ANGLE — WEATHER SCOPE CLARIFICATION

### Location: ZONE B — PREPROCESSING, ENTRY ANGLE SYSTEM

**PROBLEM IDENTIFIED:**
"Eclipse" classified as WEATHER, but eclipse is astronomical event, not meteorological.

**ADD to ENTRY ANGLE SYSTEM, under WEATH definition:**

```
WEATHER ANGLE — SCOPE DEFINITION:

WEATH(atm) = atmospheric state as opening frame

INCLUDED (directly atmospheric):
├── Meteorological: rain, fog, mist, snow, overcast, clear
├── Particle: dust storm, sandstorm, volcanic ash, smoke
├── Temperature: heat haze, frost, steam, condensation effects
├── Wind: gale, still air, turbulence markers

INCLUDED (atmospheric effects of astronomical):
├── Eclipse darkness: "Through eclipse-darkened sky..."
├── Eclipse light quality: "Under corona light..."
├── Moonlight quality: "In blue moonlight..."
├── Dawn/dusk atmosphere: "Through golden hour haze..."

EXCLUDED (astronomical positioning without atmospheric effect):
├── "Under the moon" (position, not light quality)
├── "Beneath the stars" (position, not atmosphere)
├── "During the eclipse" (temporal, not atmospheric)

PREFERRED ECLIPSE OPENINGS:

❌ TEMPORAL (belongs to TIME angle):
"During the total eclipse, a ballerina..."

⚠️ POSITIONAL (weak WEATHER):
"Under a total eclipse, a ballerina..."

✅ ATMOSPHERIC (strong WEATHER):
"Through eclipse-darkened indigo sky, a ballerina..."
"Under corona light cutting the darkened air, a ballerina..."
"In the false twilight of totality, a ballerina..."

If using astronomical event for WEATHER angle, 
emphasize ATMOSPHERIC EFFECT, not EVENT ITSELF.
```
