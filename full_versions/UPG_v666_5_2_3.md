## PATCH 6 — Camera-Visible Prose

**Location:** Section **A.2 Mandatory Quality Rules**

**Insert as new Rule 9 immediately after existing Rule 8. Output Formatting**

**Insert exactly:**
```text
9. **Camera-Visible Prose:** Every sentence in a prompt must describe
   something a camera or eye can register in a single frame. Sentences
   that describe the author's intention, the viewer's expected emotion,
   a causal mechanism that cannot be photographed, or an instruction on
   how to read the image are meta-language — delete and replace with a
   visual detail. Test: can this sentence be storyboarded as a visible
   element in one still frame? If not, it is not prompt prose. A simpler
   test: if the sentence belongs in art criticism but not in a shot list,
   rewrite it. Examples of meta-language (do not use):
   - "the scene is built around the tension between X and Y"
   - "the absence is more unsettling than presence"
   - "implied by the way the birds sit motionless"
   - "this image explores the relationship between..."
   - "the viewer is meant to feel..."
```
